5 Signs of a Romance Scammer: How to Protect Yourself

Romance scams are on the rise, with victims losing an estimated $143 million in 2018 alone. It's important to be aware of the warning signs of a potential scammer so you can protect yourself from becoming a victim. Here are five tell-tale signs to help you spot a romance scam.

1. The Scammer is Quick to Call it Love

One of the most common signs of a romance scam is when the scammer is overly eager to profess their love for you. They may send multiple love letters or text messages in a short period of time, or pressure you to move the relationship along quickly.

2. The Scammer's Profile Contains Vague or Few Images Across Platforms

Another sign of a potential scammer is if their profile contains few or vague images across multiple platforms. Scammers may use stock photos or images taken from other profiles, or may not have any photos at all.

3. The Scammer Asks for Money

One of the most obvious signs of a romance scam is when the scammer asks for money. This could be in the form of gifts, loans, or other forms of financial assistance. If the person you're talking to asks for money, it's likely a scam.

4. Allow Relationships to Develop Slowly and Naturally

It's important to take your time when getting to know someone online. Don't rush into a relationship or feel pressured to move it along quickly. Allow the relationship to develop naturally and don't be afraid to ask questions.

5. Set Up a Video Chat to Verify Their Identity

If you're talking to someone online, it's important to verify their identity. Set up a video chat to make sure the person you're talking to is who they say they are. This is a great way to make sure the person you're talking to is legitimate.

By being aware of the signs of a potential romance scam, you can protect yourself from becoming a victim. If you think you may be talking to a scammer, it's important to stop all communication and report them to the authorities.
