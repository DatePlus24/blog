5 Signs You’re Dealing with a Dating Scammer


Online dating has become increasingly popular over the past few years, and it’s no surprise why. It’s a great way to meet people from all over the world and make new connections. Unfortunately, there are also some people out there who use online dating as a way to scam unsuspecting victims. Here are five tell-tale signs to help you spot a romance scam.

1. The Scammer is Quick to Call it Love

One of the biggest red flags of a dating scammer is how quickly they declare their love for you. They may even start talking about marriage after only a few conversations. This is a major warning sign that you’re dealing with a scammer.

2. The Scammer’s Profile Contains Vague or Few Images Across Platforms

Another sign of a scammer is if their profile contains vague or few images across platforms. Scammers will often use stock photos or photos of someone else to make it look like they’re real. If you’re suspicious, you can always do a reverse image search to see if the photo is being used elsewhere.

3. The Scammer Asks for Money

One of the most obvious signs of a dating scammer is if they ask for money. They may say they need it for travel expenses, medical bills, or any other number of reasons. No matter what they say, it’s important to remember that you should never send money to someone you don’t know.

4. Allow Relationships to Develop Slowly and Naturally

If you’re looking for a real relationship, it’s important to allow it to develop slowly and naturally. Don’t rush into anything and take your time getting to know the person. If they’re pressuring you to move things along too quickly, it’s a sign that they may not be who they say they are.

5. Set Up a Video Chat to Verify Their Identity

Finally, if you’re still unsure about the person you’re talking to, you can always set up a video chat to verify their identity. This is a great way to make sure that the person you’re talking to is who they say they are.
