5 Tell-Tale Signs to Spot a Dating Scammer


Online dating can be a great way to meet new people, but it can also be a breeding ground for scammers. To protect yourself from being taken advantage of, it's important to know how to spot a dating scammer. Here are five tell-tale signs to help you identify a potential scammer.

1. The Scammer is Quick to Call it Love

One of the most common signs of a dating scammer is if they quickly profess their love for you. While it's possible to find true love online, it's highly unlikely that someone would profess their love for you after only a few conversations. If someone is claiming to be in love with you after only a few conversations, it's likely that they are a scammer.

2. The Scammer's Profile Contains Vague or Few Images Across Platforms

Another sign of a dating scammer is if their profile contains few or vague images across multiple platforms. Scammers will often use stock photos or photos of someone else to create their profile, so if the images look too good to be true, they probably are.

3. The Scammer Asks for Money

One of the most obvious signs of a dating scammer is if they ask for money. While it's possible to find someone who is willing to help you financially, it's highly unlikely that someone you've just met online would be willing to do so. If someone is asking for money, it's likely that they are a scammer.

4. Allow Relationships to Develop Slowly and Naturally

When it comes to online dating, it's important to take things slow and allow relationships to develop naturally. If someone is pushing for a relationship to move too quickly, it's likely that they are a scammer.

5. Set Up a Video Chat to Verify Their Identity

If you are suspicious of someone you are talking to online, it's a good idea to set up a video chat to verify their identity. This will help you make sure that the person you are talking to is who they say they are.

Spotting a dating scammer can be difficult, but if you know what to look for, it can be easier. If someone is quick to call it love, has few or vague images across multiple platforms, asks for money, pushes for a relationship to move too quickly, or you are suspicious of their identity, it's likely that they are a scammer. By being aware of these signs, you can protect yourself from being taken advantage of.
