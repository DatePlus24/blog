How to Protect Yourself from Video Call Scams

With the rise of technology, scammers have found new ways to target unsuspecting victims. One of the most common scams is video call scams, where fraudsters use video calls to try to get personal information or money from you. In this article, we will discuss what video call scams are, how to protect yourself from them, and what to do if you become a victim.

What Are Video Call Scams?

Video call scams are a type of fraud where scammers use video calls to try to get personal information or money from you. Scammers will often pretend to be someone you know or trust, such as a friend, family member, or even a company representative. They may also claim to be from a government agency or a charity.

Once the scammer has your attention, they will try to get you to give them money or personal information. They may also try to get you to click on a link or download a file, which could contain malicious software.

How to Protect Yourself from Video Call Scams

The best way to protect yourself from video call scams is to be aware of the warning signs. Here are some tips to help you stay safe:

• Be suspicious of any unexpected video calls. If you don't recognize the caller, don't answer the call.

• Don't give out personal information, such as your address, bank account number, or Social Security number.

• Don't click on any links or download any files sent to you during the call.

• Don't send money or gift cards to anyone you don't know.

• Don't trust anyone who claims to be from a government agency or charity.

What to Do If You Become a Victim

If you become a victim of a video call scam, there are a few steps you can take to protect yourself.

• Report the scam to the police.

• Contact your bank or credit card company to report any fraudulent charges.

• Change your passwords and update your security settings.

• Contact the FTC and file a complaint.

• Report the scam to the social media platform where the scammer contacted you.

Video call scams are becoming increasingly common, so it's important to be aware of the warning signs and take steps to protect yourself. If you become a victim of a video call scam, be sure to report it to the police and contact your bank or credit card company. By taking these steps, you can help protect yourself and others from becoming victims of fraud.
