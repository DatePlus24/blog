How to Recognize a Narcissist at the Start of a Relationship


When entering a new relationship, it can be difficult to know what to look out for. Unfortunately, narcissists can be very charming and persuasive at the beginning of a relationship, making it hard to spot the warning signs. Here are some tips to help you recognize a narcissist at the start of a relationship and handle them accordingly.

Signs to Look Out For

• Charming: Narcissists can be incredibly charming at the start of a relationship, often using flattery and compliments to make themselves seem more attractive.

• Hogging the Conversation: Narcissists often talk about themselves and their accomplishments, hogging the conversation and making it all about them.

• Feeding off Compliments: Narcissists often need a lot of validation and will feed off compliments, often expecting them from their partner.

• Lack of Empathy: Narcissists often lack empathy and are unable to understand the feelings of others.

• Few Long-Term Friends: Narcissists often don't have many (or any) long-term friends, as they often find it difficult to maintain relationships.

• Picking on You: Narcissists often pick on their partners, making jokes at their expense or belittling them.

• Gaslighting: Narcissists often use gaslighting to manipulate their partners, making them doubt their own thoughts and feelings.

Tips to Handle a Narcissist

• Set Boundaries: It is important to set boundaries with a narcissist and make sure they are respected.

• Don't Take It Personally: Narcissists often use criticism and put-downs to make themselves feel better, so it is important not to take it personally.

• Speak Up: If you feel like you are being manipulated or treated unfairly, it is important to speak up and let the narcissist know.

• Don't Engage: It is important to not engage with a narcissist when they are trying to manipulate or belittle you.

• Seek Help: If you are in a relationship with a narcissist, it is important to seek help from a professional to ensure your safety and wellbeing.

It can be difficult to recognize a narcissist at the start of a relationship, as they can be very charming and persuasive. However, it is important to be aware of the warning signs and handle them accordingly. By setting boundaries, speaking up, and seeking help, you can ensure your safety and wellbeing.
