How to Spot an Online Scammer: Common Signs and What to Do

Online scams are becoming increasingly common, and it's important to know how to spot them. Here are some common signs of an online scammer and what to do if you think you're being scammed.

Gaining Trust
One of the most common signs of an online scammer is that they will try to gain your trust in some way. They may do this by telling you a sob story or asking for help with a financial issue.

Emotional Manipulation
Another common sign of an online scammer is that they will try to emotionally manipulate you. They may try to make you feel guilty or play on your sympathy in order to get you to do something.

Asks for Action
Online scammers will often ask you to call a number, click a link, or log in to an account. They may also ask you to send money or give them access to your personal information.

Unexpected Contact
If you receive an unexpected email, text, or call from someone you don't know, it could be a scammer. Be wary of any unsolicited contact and don't give out any personal information.

Asks for Personal Info
If someone is asking for your personal information, such as your address, bank account details, or Social Security number, it's likely a scam. Never give out any of this information to someone you don't know.

Overpays You
If someone overpays you for something, it could be a sign of a scam. Be wary of any payments that are much higher than the amount you agreed upon.

Promises Something
If someone is promising you something that seems too good to be true, it probably is. Be wary of any promises that seem too good to be true.

Wire Transfer Request
If someone is asking you to wire money to them, it's almost certainly a scam. Never wire money to someone you don't know.

It's important to be aware of the common signs of an online scammer. If you think you're being scammed, don't give out any personal information or money and contact the authorities.
