How to Write a Good First Message on a Dating App: Tips and Examples

Sending the perfect first message can be the key to getting a response on a dating app. Check out these tips and examples to help you craft a great first message that will catch their attention.

When it comes to online dating, the first message you send is crucial. It's the first impression you make and can determine whether or not you get a response. So, how do you write a good first message that will catch their attention and make them want to respond? Here are some tips and examples to help you get started.

  1. Personalize your message: Avoid generic messages like "Hey, what's up?" and take the time to read their profile and find something to comment on or ask about.

  2. Keep it short and sweet: Your first message should be concise and to the point. Avoid long-winded messages that may come across as desperate or creepy.

  3. Use proper grammar and spelling: It may seem obvious, but using proper grammar and spelling can make a big difference. It shows that you took the time to craft a thoughtful message and can make you appear more intelligent and attractive.

  4. Show your personality: Don't be afraid to be yourself and show off your unique personality in your first message. Use humor or wit to stand out and make them laugh.

  5. Ask a question: End your message with a question to encourage a response and keep the conversation going. This shows that you're interested in getting to know them and can help break the ice.

Here are some examples of good first messages:

  • "I noticed we both love hiking. Have you been on any great hikes recently? I'm always looking for new trail recommendations."

  • "I see you're a fan of sushi. Do you have a favorite sushi restaurant in the area? I'm always on the lookout for good sushi."

  • "I couldn't help but notice your love for travel. What's your favorite place you've visited so far? I'm planning my next trip and looking for inspiration."

Writing a good first message on a dating app takes a little effort and thought, but it can make all the difference. By personalizing your message, keeping it short and sweet, using proper grammar and spelling, showing your personality, and asking a question, you can increase your chances of getting a response and starting a conversation with a potential match.
