The Art of Tuning: Understanding the Pre-Flirtation Game

Tuning is a form of flirting that takes place before the actual process of flirting begins. It is a way of testing the waters, so to speak, and gauging the level of interest before making a more serious commitment.

"Keeping Things at a Level of Plausible Deniability"

Tuning involves keeping things at a level of plausible deniability. This means that the tuner is being subtle and indirect, and not putting themselves too far out there. They are essentially sending out feelers to see if the other person is interested before making a more overt move.

"LAYBY - The Pre-Flirtation Stage"

Tuning is often referred to as the LAYBY stage of flirting. It is the stage before flirting itself and it is meant to be a low-risk way of testing the waters. If nothing comes to pass, the tuner hasn't put themselves in a vulnerable position. However, if the other person responds positively to the tuning, it can lead to a more serious flirtation.

"When the Tuner is Still in a Relationship"

It is worth mentioning that tuning can also take place when the tuner is still in a relationship. In these cases, tuning can be seen as unethical and can lead to complications if the relationship becomes serious.

In conclusion, tuning is a way of gauging interest before making a more serious commitment. It is a form of pre-flirtation and is meant to be a low-risk way of testing the waters. While it can be a harmless and even playful part of the flirting process, it is important to consider the consequences when the tuner is still in a relationship.
