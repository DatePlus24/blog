The Dangers of Online Dating: Fraud, Stalking and Sexual Abuse


Online dating has become increasingly popular over the last decade, with more and more people turning to the internet to find their perfect match. Unfortunately, this also means that online dating has become a target for fraudsters, scammers, stalkers and sexual predators. Here, we will look at the risks associated with online dating and how to protect yourself from them.

Online Dating Fraud

Online dating fraud is one of the most common risks associated with online dating. Scammers exploit people looking for romantic partners, often via dating websites, apps or social media by pretending to be prospective companions. They play on emotional triggers to get you to provide money, gifts or personal details.

Common signs of online dating fraud include:

• Asking for money or financial assistance
• Making excuses for not meeting in person
• Asking for personal information such as your address or bank details
• Not being able to provide proof of identity
• Making requests for gifts or favors

Stalking

Stalking is another risk associated with online dating. Stalkers use online dating sites to find and target potential victims, often by creating fake profiles or by posing as someone else. They may use the information they find to harass or threaten their victims, or even to commit identity theft.

Signs of stalking include:

• Unwanted contact, such as emails, text messages or phone calls
• Unwanted gifts or messages
• Following or tracking you online or in person
• Threatening behavior or language

Sexual Abuse

Sexual abuse is another risk associated with online dating. Predators may use online dating sites to find and target potential victims, often by creating fake profiles or by posing as someone else. They may use the information they find to harass or threaten their victims, or even to commit sexual assault.

Signs of sexual abuse include:

• Unwanted sexual advances or requests
• Pressure to engage in sexual activities
• Unwanted physical contact
• Threats of physical or sexual violence

Protecting Yourself

To protect yourself from the dangers of online dating, it is important to be aware of the risks and take steps to protect yourself. Here are some tips to help you stay safe:

• Always meet in a public place for the first few dates
• Don’t give out personal information such as your address or bank details
• Do a background check on the person you are meeting
• Don’t respond to threatening or abusive messages
• Report any suspicious activity to the dating site or app

Online dating can be a great way to meet new people, but it is important to be aware of the risks associated with it. By taking steps to protect yourself, you can help ensure that your online dating experience is safe and enjoyable.
