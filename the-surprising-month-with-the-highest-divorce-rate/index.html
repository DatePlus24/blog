The Surprising Month with the Highest Divorce Rate


Most people assume that January is the month with the highest divorce rate, but a recent study has revealed that this is not the case. While there could be many reasons for this, the study by FindLaw and Westlaw found that the month with the highest divorce rate is actually March.

Reasons for the High Divorce Rate in March

• Stressful Holiday Season: Many couples decide to wait until after the holiday season to file for divorce. This is because they don't want to damper the holiday season for family and friends by announcing a divorce.

• Financial Reasons: The holiday season can be expensive and couples may wait until after the holidays to file for divorce when they have more financial stability.

• New Year’s Resolutions: Some couples may wait until after the holidays to make a New Year’s resolution to end their marriage.

• Desire for One More Family Holiday: Some couples may wait until after the holidays to file for divorce so they can enjoy one more family holiday together.

• Tax Reasons: Couples may wait until after the holidays to file for divorce so they can take advantage of tax benefits.

• More Time to Prepare: Couples may wait until after the holidays to file for divorce so they have more time to prepare emotionally and financially.

While many people assume that January is the month with the highest divorce rate, a recent study by FindLaw and Westlaw found that the month with the highest divorce rate is actually March.
