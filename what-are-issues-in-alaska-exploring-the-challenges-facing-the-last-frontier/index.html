What Are Issues in Alaska? Exploring the Challenges Facing the Last Frontier


Alaska is a vast and beautiful land of rugged mountains, pristine forests, and vast tundra. But beneath its beauty lies a variety of issues that threaten the lives and livelihoods of its inhabitants. From erosion and flooding to thawing permafrost and climate change, Alaska is facing a number of challenges that could have serious consequences for its future.

Erosion, Flooding, and Thawing Permafrost

Erosion, flooding, and thawing permafrost are all issues that are particularly concerning in Alaska. Erosion can cause significant damage to infrastructure, including roads, bridges, and buildings. Flooding can also cause damage to infrastructure and can threaten lives, especially in Alaska Native villages.

Thawing permafrost is another issue that is of particular concern in Alaska. Permafrost is soil that remains frozen year-round and is a key component of the Arctic environment. As temperatures rise, the permafrost thaws, which can lead to flooding, erosion, and damage to infrastructure. In Alaska Native villages, thawing permafrost can threaten lives and damage homes and other structures.

Climate Change

Climate change is another issue that is of particular concern in Alaska. As temperatures rise, the effects of erosion, flooding, and thawing permafrost are expected to worsen. In addition, rising temperatures can lead to an increase in wildfires, which can cause significant damage to forests and other ecosystems.
