What is the unhappiest city in America?


When it comes to happiness, not all cities are created equal. A recent analysis of data including factors such as personal finance, well-being and quality of life has named Birmingham, Alabama as the least happy city in the U.S. Smartasset.com said it came to the determination after analyzing statistics from the 200 largest cities in the country.

What Makes Birmingham the Unhappiest City in America?

Smartasset.com used a variety of data points to determine the unhappiest city in America. These included the unemployment rate, poverty rate, average commute time, and housing affordability. Birmingham scored the lowest in all of these categories, making it the least happy city in the U.S.

Unemployment Rate: Birmingham had an unemployment rate of 6.3%, which is significantly higher than the national average of 4.4%.

Poverty Rate: Birmingham also had a poverty rate of 28.3%, which is more than double the national average of 12.3%.

Average Commute Time: The average commute time in Birmingham was 25.2 minutes, which is longer than the national average of 24.3 minutes.

Housing Affordability: Birmingham had a housing affordability score of 6.9, which is lower than the national average of 7.2.

Other Factors Contributing to Unhappiness in Birmingham

In addition to the data points mentioned above, there are other factors that contribute to the unhappiness of Birmingham residents. These include a lack of access to quality healthcare, a lack of access to quality education, and a lack of economic opportunity.
