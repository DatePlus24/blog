Who Moves Faster After Breakup?


When it comes to recovering from a breakup, the research indicates that men take longer time than women and struggle more to move on. In fact, the researchers observed that many male participants suffered from PRG (Post Relationship Grief) at the time of the study even if they had parted ways more than a year ago.

Men Take Longer to Recover

Men tend to take longer to recover from a breakup than women. This is due to the fact that men are more likely to experience strong feelings of grief and loss, as well as difficulty in letting go of the relationship. In addition, men may be more likely to engage in rumination, which is the tendency to keep thinking about the relationship and the breakup, and to re-experience the emotions associated with it.

Women Tend to Move On Quicker

In contrast, women are more likely to move on quicker after a breakup. This is because women are more likely to seek social support and to talk about their feelings with friends and family. This helps them to process their emotions and to come to terms with the breakup. In addition, women are more likely to engage in positive activities such as exercising, which can help them to distract themselves from their emotions and to move on with their lives.

The Role of Time

Time is also an important factor in recovery from a breakup. As time passes, both men and women are likely to find it easier to move on. This is because the emotions associated with the breakup will gradually fade and the person will be able to focus on the present and the future.

Overall, the research indicates that men take longer to recover from a breakup than women. This is due to the fact that men are more likely to experience strong feelings of grief and loss, as well as difficulty in letting go of the relationship. In contrast, women are more likely to move on quicker after a breakup due to their tendency to seek social support and to engage in positive activities. Time is also an important factor in recovery from a breakup, as the emotions associated with the breakup will gradually fade as time passes.
